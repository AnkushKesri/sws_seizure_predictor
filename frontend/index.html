About This Tool

This tool applies machine learning to predict seizure onset in patients diagnosed with Sturge-Weber Syndrome (SWS), a rare neurocutaneous disorder. Predictions are based on five clinically obtained binary features and are intended to support, not replace, physician judgment.

How It Works

Three model architectures (Random Forest, Gaussian SVC, Linear SVC) are each trained across five cross-validation folds, yielding 15 models total. Predictions use a two-stage ensemble: averaging within each model type across folds, then averaging across the three types for the final probability estimate.

Important Limitations

This tool is validated on a specific SWS patient cohort and results may not generalize to all populations. The output is a probabilistic estimate and must be interpreted in the context of the full clinical picture by a qualified healthcare provider.
